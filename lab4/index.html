Task 1: Pasta Pseudocode

1. Prepare Mushrooms, Garlic, and Shallot
1.1 Remove any outside shell/skin from garlic and shallot
1.2 Slice one clove in thin slices
1.3 Dice the rest of garlic into small pieces
1.4 Slice mushrooms into small slices
2. Prepare Boiling Water for Pasta
2.1 Fill pot with warm water
2.2 Heavily salt the water
2.3 Place the pot, covered, on high until boiling
3. Prepare Chicken and Mushrooms
3.1 Make any necessary trimmings to chicken
3.2 Season chicken on both sides with user choice of seasonings
3.3 Season mushroom slices with salt and pepper
3.4 Lightly toss mushrooms to spread seasoning
4. Cook Mushrooms
4.1 Place mushrooms in warm pan on medium heat
4.2 Let mushrooms release water and cook until they have shrunk and become a darker color.
4.3 Place mushrooms in dish to the side
5. Pour Pasta in Boiling Water
6. Cook Chicken
6.1 Throw sliced garlic into oil in a hot pan and let sit until oil is thin
6.2 Place chicken into pan and let sit for 6 minutes
Once chicken starts to show some browning on one side, flip and repeat the process
6.3 Once chicken has been cooked through and has hit a reasonable temp, turn up heat to give chicken a light crust
6.4 Once chicken is fully cooked, place into dish/cutting board and cover with foil
7. Cook Sauce
7.1 In a warm pan place minced garlic and oil
7.2 When garlic is fragrant, put into pan either user choice of red sauce or a combination of tomato paste and canned stewed tomatoes
7.3 Season red sauce, optionally add in chili flakes
7.4 Add butter, heavy whipping cream, and parmesan until sauce is as creamy as needed.
8. Dish
8.1 When cooked, strain out user choice of pasta then add to sauce
8.2 Add to sauce mushrooms and chicken, mix into pasta until one dish
8.3 Place onto dish for eating

Challenge

The main challenge of this lab was to create lines of pseudocode that could help us get into the habit of thinking like a coder might. First, we had to pseudocode and everyday task, which forced us to think of our usual tasks like how a coder might. Then, we had to pseudocode a simple computer game.

Problems

One of the main problems I ran into during this lab was formatting a large amount of text such as our pseudocoding text. While I could make it legible and easy to understand, there was still a fair amount of text that seemed randomly indented. Additionally I struggled to make the text fit next to eachother in CSS for a cleaner profile.

Reflection

This assignment was fairly easy because I did not need to make much happen on any of the sites themselves. I found it easy to convert the way I thought about tasks into the way that coders think about coding. I found it super fun to think of a way to build a rock paper scissors game, and I tried hard to make the steps be similar to what one would need to code a RPS game.
